What We Do

OUR AMAZING SERVICES

Curriculum Development, Integration and Implementation

Novamite develops affordable and innovative educational resources (e.g. curricula and materials). We collaborate with schools and nonprofit organizations to integrate our curricula into current educational programs

Increased Access to Educational Opportunities

Novamite connects talented youth participating in coding educational programs with advanced and hands-on opportunities (e.g. tech-mentor programs and internships in tech-related environments)

Fostering of a Supportive Community

Novamite promotes a community of social and environmental responsibility through mentorship programs and community events

Our Team

THE DREAM TEAM

Johanna Garcia

CEO

Johanna Garcia

CEO

Johanna was born and raised in Bogota, Colombia. She finished her undergraduate in Electronics Engineering, and moved to the United States in 2007. She continued her graduate studies in Electrical Engineering for Image and Digital Signal Processing in CalPoly Pomona. Having the opportunity to work at JPL (Jet Propulsion Laboratory), opened career prospects for Johanna in the aerospace field. Currently, she is a FPGA design engineer for space video control systems. During her career, Johanna has noticed the lack of diversity and has experienced some of the difficulties common to underrepresented individuals in the engineering fields . She wants to motivate youth to become great engineers, and thus help their families have higher standards of living.

Laura Garcia

WEB DESIGNER

Laura Garcia

Web Designer

Laura was born and raised in Bogota, Colombia. She moved to the United States in 2007 and has since pursued a career in Clinical Psychology. She is currently a PhD student and a National Science Foundation (NSF) fellow at the University of Southern California (USC). Laura has always been fascinated by technology and mental health. As an undergrad, she worked with at-risk youth in a photo-voice program, which allowed youth to appreciate and improve their environments by taking meaningful photos of their community. She also became an active member of nonprofit organizations serving the Latino community in the San Bernardino county through a variety of educational programs. At USC, she is currently pursuing both of her main interests by conducting research on the use of virtual humans for clinical interviewing. Most importantly, through her experiences, Laura noticed the importance of contextual factors in mental health. Through her work at Novamite, she strives to help disadvantaged communities strengthen their psychological coping skills while simultaneously increasing their opportunities to improve their overall quality of life.

Oswald Jones

DEVELOPER

Oswald Jones

Developer

Oz has worked as the director of engineering for multiple startups. As a kid Oz loved movies about hackers. He would spend hours studying programming books at home but get nowhere. The frustration from his failed attempts and feelings of disconnect from his classmates led Oz to drop out of High School. With the support of his Mexican mother and his American father, Oz was able to accomplish his dream of becoming a programmer. However, after landing a job in the technology leadership program of JP Morgan Chase, Oz realized that he and many of his peers were still not that good at programming. It took years of hard work in corporations such as Microsoft, Disney, Fox and Intuit, for Oz to finally feel comfortable as a senior engineer. It was then when a bootcamp company offered him a job to teach code to adults. During this process, he realized that it would be better to teach the basics of coding to youth. Nine years of engineering experience has proven to him that code has become the new literacy of the 21st century. For this reason, Oz joined Novamite, as he shares the belief that everyone has the ability and the right to learn code.
